The FreeBSD and Apple logos are quite similar. <br/>
Both have a heavy base and leaves / tails on top.

![Apple and FreeBSD logos compared by their heavy base and top detail, followed by a wrong circular crop approach](apple-freebsd-wrong-circle-fit.jpg)

<br/>
<br/>

## This is how Apple fits its logo into a circle

![Apple logo centered on the circular top surface of a black HomePod](apple-homepod-logo.jpg)

<br/>

With guides:

![Apple HomePod top with orange circular guides around the speaker surface and Apple logo](apple-homepod-circle-guides.jpg)


<br/>
<br/>

Apple tries to keep heavy center closer to the surrounded surface:

![Apple logo on the HomePod with orange guides showing its mass placed close to the center surface](apple-logo-mass-guide.jpg)

<br/>
<br/>

So the mass aligned to the center:
![Blurred Apple logo mass aligned inside an orange center circle on the HomePod](apple-logo-mass-center-blur.jpg)

<br/>

## We can apply that same logic to the FreeBSD logo too

![FreeBSD logo on the HomePod with orange guides showing the same center-mass approach](freebsd-logo-mass-guide.jpg)

![Blurred FreeBSD logo mass aligned inside an orange center circle on the HomePod](freebsd-logo-mass-center-blur.jpg)

![FreeBSD logo fitted cleanly into the circular center of the HomePod](freebsd-logo-final-circle-fit.jpg)
